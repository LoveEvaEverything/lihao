Survivor calls for ban on spy cameras
Sandy was covertly filmed in a bathroom in a gross breach of privacy and is calling on the Government to ban the import and sale of spy cameras. John Harford

They’ve been hidden in bedside alarm clocks, air fresheners and smoke alarms - everyday electronics which may appear innocuous, but house tiny spy cameras that can film private moments.

Police allege Sandy* was secretly filmed in a bathroom by a man she once considered a close friend. The man has been charged by police.

She’s calling on the Government to ban the import, sale and possession of spy cameras - which are available for sale in New Zealand.
